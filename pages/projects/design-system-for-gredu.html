Design System for Gredu (EdTech Platform)

CLIENT

Gredu

MY ROLE

  • UI/UX Designer
  • Also got in charge of:
  • Design System

DURATION

4 months

YEAR

2020

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Gredu is an EdTech Startup that focused on integrating the school environment for teachers, parents, and students.
At that time, we have several products handled by several different designers and engineers.
In this project, I got in charge of build initial design system along with Project Lead.

MAIN GOAL

Get everything aligned, has a consistent design quality in all product even it handled by different designers and enginees, and good accessibility.

TOOLS

Sketch App, Abstract, InVision, Jira, Trello, and Miro.

The background

At that time, our product environment started to evolve. The team got bigger and all of our product handled by several different designers and engineers. To make sure that we’re all aligned, we tried to review the product we produce and our current workflow.

Review with Lightning Decision Jam (LDJ) approach

We did this during our weekly design sync-up. Started with “things that are working” before “capture all problems”. This method keeps our focus on problem-solving in the next step instead of only complaining and criticism. Ended up with some actionable solutions to be prioritized.

Project Challenges

  • Each product has a distinguished personality, e.g., for teacher, for student, and for parent.
  • Work on this project while still have to finish tasks from PMs without special deadline adjustment.

Design system: Initial scale

The team need a design system that sets the guideline for designers, engineers, and writers to work and collaborate faster yet produce high-quality deliverables. However, it was way too big and unrealistic at that moment to start with all products at the same time. We decide to start with the Student App (iOS & Android) for the first MVP with a scalable foundation for the rest of the product line up.

Implement design foundation to student app as a starter

As a starting point, we aimed to develop the foundation and refine existing student app components.

Collecting and curating components

Probably, this is the most tedious part, we have to dive into every single artboards to collect and sort current assets ranging from icons, illustrations, buttons, forms, type sizes, colors, etc.
Here are some examples of problem from UI perspective:

  • Colors: redundant color shades and color contrast didn't meet WCAG 2.1 standard.
  • Icons: inconsistent style, not using bound, and redundant icons.
  • Typography: no standard type scale and contrast issue.
  • Components: inconsistent style, accessibility issue, and no standard pattern/behaviour.

Define the guidelines for cross-platform design

Ideally, each platform should get tailored design since they have their own interaction pattern. However, due to the lack of resources, the team decided to go with unified components that fit both Android and iOS app with minimum adjustment as possible.

The Result

Components in nested symbols

With this structure, we just have to make the changes in foundations and respective components then the rest will be updated. This way everything will easier to maintain and make designers can focus on problem-solving and innovation rather than doing tiring repetitive works.

Refined colors

Divide the kind of layer style into base, tint, container, border, scrim, and transparent to fit the need. This version was met the WCAG 2.1 standard.

Refined icons

Unified icon style, grouped into relevant categories, and defined icon specs.

Sample of refined components: Buttons

Complete the needed button sizes, completed type of button, completed button states, and defined how and when to use each of type.

Sample of refined components: Input Text

Provided every possible states of input text.

Achievement summary

  1. Created solid baseline for growing design system.
  2. Set guidelines and conventions (naming, size, spacing, etc).
  3. Improved overall UI of the app (student app).
  4. Set new workflow and rules for design revision and handoff.
  5. Fasten design process.
  6. Put designers and engineers on the same page for easier tracking (units, naming, versioning, etc).

Learnings

  1. Show don't tell, do the extra miles to getting stakeholder taste the benefits.
  2. The convention would really help our process in the next round to keep aligned.
  3. Single source of truth makes us stay in the line and no more unnecessary version created.
  4. A design system should meet the needs of your company, don't have to be the same as others.
