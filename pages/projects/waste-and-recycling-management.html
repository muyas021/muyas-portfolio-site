Waste and Recycling Management

CLIENT

TBA

MY ROLE

  • User Research
  • Visual Design
  • Interaction Design

DURATION

4.5 months

YEAR

2020

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Bootstrapped a Startup that focused on waste and recycling management.
The core team are Kenrick Setiobudi (Commercial Lead & PM), Franco Octavianus (Technology Lead), and Muyas—me (Design, UI & UX Lead).

MAIN GOAL

Find the real problem, prioritise solution to execute in initial, and MVP of the waste management system.

TOOLS

Figma, Marvel, Jira, Confluence, and Adobe Photoshop.

Understand the root

Sharpen our knowledge using desk research

In this initial part, I did desk research with curating information from some report. The purpose is to get familiar with the problem and find additional data to support the user interview.

Get to know deeply using user interview

To get things right, I need to know the perspective of each side inside that involved in waste management—in this case, urban consumer and recycler. With the help of know data from desk research, I set up the objective to be the focus of observation in the interview.

Research objective:

  • Understand the urban consumer behaviour in treating rubbish
  • Understand the recycler behaviour in recycling rubbish

Treat the insight as a trigger to open up ideas

Spot the insight using affinity mapping

After did desk research and user interview, I tried to connect findings and key findings to find the insights. This session really helps us to know what happened in the field and why it happened to give a useful recommendation for next phase.

Reframe the insight as standardized challenges using How Might We (HMW) approach

From the insights that we have found, I tried to let us open for any possibility to innovate using How Might We (HMW) approach.

Prioritise solution to execute on

By answering the HMW, I ended up with some ideas. Then, I prioritised some ideas to be solved in MVP by considering the impact and effort.

In estimating the impact and effort, we focused on these indicators below:

  • Usability
  • Design
  • Development
  • Business goal

Note: If you see some blurry image below, it’s not your browser or your eyes. It’s me hiding some future strategy.

The MVP Product

Take some steps forward, I came up with this prototype for our MVP after did some experiment for the most delightful experience that matches with our first adopter.

Why a mobile app?

In this MVP, we focused on our first time adopter—urban consumer. We got some data that this group of people have a lot of time interacting with smartphone. So, we tried to encourage them to join along in solving this problem. Also, we believe this would work because we provide a reward scheme for each rubbish they recycle.

Dark mode version

This fancy dark mode version is our approach to tell everyone about our identity as a company that wanna solve this issue with technology.

Note: We covered Android and iOS app version, but I only showcasing the iOS version at this time.

Outcomes

  1. A valid problem that need to solve based on research from real user.
  2. The MVP Product with strong identity.
  3. Insight from usability test (published soon).

Learnings

  1. The desk research really helps us to sharpen our knowledge about the topic.
  2. Knowing from user help us see a lot of perspectives and something we never know about the real condition in the field. It prevents us to solve the wrong problem.
  3. The way to probing in the interview and connecting findings become insights are the most crucial part that leads us to solve the right problem.
  4. Knowing the target user behaviour can help you decide the kind platform that can be the best suit for providing the solution.
